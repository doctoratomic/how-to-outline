How to Outline


I've been a writer for a long time. I do find that the best methods to streamlining making good creative content is to challenge yourself to make something within the confines of structure.

To some, that might sound like the antithesis to creativity. But challenging that notion is what will ultimately make you a great writer. And a great storyteller.

R.L. Stein told a story in his Master Class about a friend of his who was a famous and well respected, brilliant writer. This person declared that he "only wrote for himself, and that he considers nothing else when approaching a writing project." To which Mr. Stein replies, "Well that's why you're a great writer, and I'm rich!"

I think about this quote a lot. Though I'm not sure if I got the story exactly. But this was in a video referring to thinking about the audience and what they want out of you as a writer.
